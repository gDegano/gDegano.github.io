ICU-EEG Pattern Detection

Read the publication

The goal of this project was to assess the performance of a lightweight CNN for the detection of abnormal EEG patterns in critical care settings.

The Details

I will not go into many details regarding the training database and all the preprocessing steps, BUT a bunch of things are worth mentioning:

1. The CNN was trained on 19 channels so that it can be easily applied to the standard 10-20 EEG montage used in ICUs. 2. Six different patterns were included in the training (LPD, GPD, GRDA, LRDA, seizures and other) and the network was trained to output a probability for 50s windows of EEG. 3. The code is ready on GitHub but for hospital reasons I can only share it after a email request. 4. Chunks of ideas were developed from the work of Chris Deotte.

The above figure shows that the input images was a concatenation of the spectrograms of the chains including transverse and double banana montages. Then a simple normalization was addedd and finally all was plugged in the CNN.
